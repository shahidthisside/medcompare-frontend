Health Tips & Common Medicines

Maintaining a Healthy Immune System

A strong immune system is your body's first line of defense against infections and illnesses. Here are some tips to boost your immunity:

  • Get adequate sleep (7-9 hours for adults)
  • Eat a balanced diet rich in fruits and vegetables
  • Exercise regularly
  • Stay hydrated
  • Manage stress through meditation or other relaxation techniques
  • Avoid smoking and limit alcohol consumption

Vitamin C Supplements

Usage: Vitamin C is an antioxidant that helps strengthen the immune system. It's commonly taken to prevent or reduce the duration of the common cold.

Typical Dosage: 500-1000mg daily for adults. Always follow the dosage instructions on the product label.

Zinc Supplements

Usage: Zinc supports immune function and may help reduce the duration of cold symptoms when taken at the onset of illness.

Typical Dosage: 15-30mg daily for adults during illness. Long-term use should be limited to 15mg daily.

Managing Fever and Pain

Fever is a common symptom of many illnesses and is actually a sign that your body is fighting an infection. Here's how to manage fever and associated pain:

  • Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of fluids
  • Rest to help your body recover
  • Use a light blanket if you have chills
  • Take a lukewarm bath to help reduce fever
  • Consult a doctor if fever persists for more than three days or exceeds 103°F (39.4°C)

Paracetamol (Acetaminophen)

Usage: Used to reduce fever and relieve mild to moderate pain from headaches, muscle aches, and colds.

Typical Dosage: For adults, 500-1000mg every 4-6 hours as needed, not exceeding 4000mg in 24 hours.

Ibuprofen

Usage: A nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) used to reduce fever, pain, and inflammation.

Typical Dosage: For adults, 200-400mg every 4-6 hours as needed, not exceeding 1200mg in 24 hours unless directed by a doctor.

Digestive Health

Maintaining good digestive health is essential for overall wellbeing. Here are some tips for a healthy digestive system:

  • Eat a high-fiber diet including fruits, vegetables, and whole grains
  • Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water
  • Exercise regularly to promote healthy digestion
  • Eat slowly and chew food thoroughly
  • Manage stress, which can affect digestive function
  • Limit fatty foods, alcohol, and caffeine

Antacids

Usage: Used to neutralize stomach acid and provide relief from heartburn, acid indigestion, and upset stomach.

Typical Usage: Take as directed on the product label, usually after meals and at bedtime.

Probiotics

Usage: Supplements containing beneficial bacteria that help maintain a healthy gut microbiome.

Typical Usage: Follow product directions, usually one capsule daily with a meal.
